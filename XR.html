3a. Ideas for the internal battery

The board is ready to ride as is, but it doesn’t have the same oomph as before. You can order a replacement stock-style battery – simply unplug a couple of connectors and drop in the new battery. There are various sources in Europe for some very nice custom-made packs.

3b. Ideas for the range extender

There’s a range extender port (VnR) installed on this board. It’s a standard tap into the battery cable, allowing you to add another 40V battery in parallel for longer range. For example, Greenworks 40V tool batteries work well – available in variants such as 4Wh or 6Wh. You can even charge both batteries simultaneously using the XR charger.

A few cautions: never connect a battery that isn’t within about ±10% of the internal battery’s charge; and if you leave the Onewheel App running while riding, you may encounter an “incompatible hardware” error. (A power cycle usually fixes that.)

Want to remove the range extender? There are “bullet” plugs mid-cable for a modular setup, or you can remove the “spade” plugs near the wire tap for a more permanent removal – all solder-free.
